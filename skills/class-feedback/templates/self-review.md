# Self-review: [class name / date]

**What the class was trying to do.** [State the intended arc, theme, and peak in
one line so the critique has a target.]

**What worked, and why.** [Name the specific moves that earned the result.
Mechanism, not vibes. "The X prep set up the Y peak because..." Not "nice flow."]

**What did not land.** [Be direct. Pacing, a prep that didn't support the peak, a
cue that confused, an energy dip, a transition that broke. Say WHERE and WHY.]

**Sequencing audit.**
- Did the warm-up earn the peak? [Which ingredients did the peak need, and were
  they all rehearsed?]
- Anything unsafe or under-prepped? [General structural flags only — no invented
  injuries or diagnoses.]
- Did repetition serve a purpose or pad the clock?
- Both sides: where did the second side diverge, and was that intentional?
- Was the cool-down enough to down-regulate?

**Cueing audit.**
- Were cues verb + body part + direction, or vague? [Cite the vague ones.]
- Too many words anywhere?
- Did the theme get taught, or just announced?

**Evidence from reviews / voice note.** [If reviews or a post-class voice memo
are in the inputs, fold the patterns in here as evidence — already read for the
need underneath the words. Tie a change to the pattern without reprinting raw
complaints. Skip this section if there's no such input.]

**The one change.** [If you change only one thing next time, what is it and why?
Concrete action, tied to the pattern that supports it.]

**Keep.** [The one or two things working well that should not be touched.]
